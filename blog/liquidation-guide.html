What Is Liquidation Price?

Liquidation price is the market price at which your leveraged position is automatically closed by the exchange. This happens when your margin can no longer cover unrealized losses.

Liquidation vs Stop Loss

A stop loss is a trader-controlled exit. Liquidation is an exchange-controlled exit. The key difference is that liquidation usually occurs at the worst possible price, often during high volatility.

Professional traders always exit trades via stop loss â€” never liquidation.

How Leverage Affects Liquidation Price

Higher leverage brings the liquidation price closer to your entry. At 50x leverage, even a 2% adverse price move can wipe out your position.

Lower leverage creates more breathing room, allowing trades to survive normal market fluctuations.
