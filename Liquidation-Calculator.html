Liquidation Calculator – Crypto Margin Trading Tool

What is this Liquidation Calculator?

This crypto liquidation calculator helps margin traders determine the liquidation price for leveraged positions. Avoid unexpected losses by combining this tool with the Stop Loss Calculator, Leverage Calculator, and Risk Reward Calculator.

How to Use This Calculator

Enter your entry price, leverage, and margin type. Click "Calculate" to get the estimated liquidation price. Use it to protect your account from forced liquidation.

Liquidation Calculator Example

If your entry price is $100, leverage is 5x, and your account equity is $500, the estimated liquidation price is:

Liquidation Price = Entry × (1 – Equity ÷ (Position × Leverage))

Example: $100 × (1 – 500 ÷ (100 × 5)) = $0 (simplified example)

This shows how leverage can magnify risk and why monitoring liquidation levels is essential.

Instructions & FAQ

1. What is liquidation?

Liquidation occurs when a leveraged position loses enough value that the exchange automatically closes it to prevent further losses.

2. How do I use this calculator?

Enter entry price, leverage, and account equity to calculate estimated liquidation price.

3. Why is risk management important for margin trading?

Leverage increases both potential profits and losses. Monitoring liquidation helps prevent account wipeouts.

4. Can I use stop loss with liquidation?

Yes, use the Stop Loss Calculator to limit losses before reaching liquidation.

5. How does leverage affect liquidation?

Higher leverage lowers the liquidation threshold. Even small price movements can trigger liquidation.

6. Can I combine with risk/reward analysis?

Yes, use Risk Reward Calculator to evaluate trades while considering liquidation risk.

7. Is this suitable for all crypto assets?

Yes, adjust inputs based on asset volatility and exchange margin requirements.

8. Recommended leverage for beginners?

Use low leverage (2x–5x) to minimize liquidation risk.

9. Does this include fees?

No, fees are not included. Consider them separately when planning trades.

10. How to prevent liquidation?

Use appropriate stop losses, monitor margin, and avoid excessive leverage. This calculator helps estimate safe levels.
